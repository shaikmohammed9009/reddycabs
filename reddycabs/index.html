The Divine Witness

Sakshi Ganapati, literally meaning "Witness Ganesha," holds a unique and sacred position in Hindu temple traditions, particularly at Srisailam. As the divine witness, Lord Ganesha observes and sanctifies all religious ceremonies, prayers, and devotional activities within the temple complex.

Sacred Validation

In Hindu tradition, Sakshi Ganapati is invoked as the celestial witness to all sacred vows, marriages, and important life ceremonies.

Divine Approval

His presence ensures that all spiritual activities are conducted with divine approval and blessing from the cosmic realm.

Significance at Srisailam Temple

Divine Origin Story

Lord Mallikarjuna Swamy manifested when Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati took the forms of Mallikarjuna and Bhramaramba respectively. According to legend, when their sons Ganesha and Kartikeya competed to marry first, Ganesha won by circumambulating his parents. Hurt by this, Kartikeya went to Krauncha mountain. To console him, Shiva and Parvati appeared as Mallikarjuna and Bhramaramba at Srisailam.

Ancient Scriptures

The glory of Lord Mallikarjuna is extensively described in Skanda Purana, Linga Purana, and Shiva Purana. The temple finds mention in Mahabharata as one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites.

Royal Patronage

Historical records show patronage from Satavahanas, Ikshvakus, Pallavas, Cholas, Kakatiyas, and Vijayanagara rulers. Krishna Deva Raya made significant contributions to the temple.

Sacred Significance of Lord Mallikarjuna

  • Jyotirlinga: One of 12 self-manifested Shiva lingas representing infinite light
  • Dual Deity: Both Shiva (Mallikarjuna) and Shakti (Bhramaramba) reside here
  • Moksha Giver: Believed that darshan here grants liberation from cycle of birth-death
  • Kailash of South: Known as "Dakshina Kailasam" - Southern abode of Lord Shiva
  • Miraculous Powers: Devotees believe prayers here fulfill desires and cure ailments

Divine Manifestation

Bhramaramba Devi is the divine consort of Lord Mallikarjuna at Srisailam. She manifested herself to console Lord Kartikeya when he went to Krauncha mountain. As the better half of Lord Shiva, she took the form of Bhramaramba to reside alongside Mallikarjuna at this sacred Shakti Peetha.

Shakti Peetha

Srisailam is one of the most important among the 18 Shakti Peethas, where the goddess's throat is believed to have fallen, making it supremely sacred.

Bhramari Vidya

The goddess is the epicenter of Naada Vidya (sound knowledge) and Bhramari Vidya, where devotees can hear divine sounds by placing their ears at her temple's backside.

Sacred Significance of Bhramaramba Devi

  • Mother of Three Worlds: Protector and nurturer of all three realms
  • Wish Fulfiller: No deity matches her power to grant devotees' desires
  • Divine Blessings: Grants long life, success, health, prosperity and children
  • Knowledge Giver: Source of all Vedas and divine wisdom
  • Fear Destroyer: Destroys enemies, fears and grants protection

About Srisailam Dam

Srisailam Dam is a magnificent hydroelectric project built across the Krishna River in Andhra Pradesh. Completed in 1981, it stands as one of the largest capacity working hydroelectric stations in India. The dam not only generates clean energy but also creates a stunning reservoir that enhances the natural beauty of the sacred Srisailam region.

Construction Period

Construction began in 1960 and was completed in 1981, taking over two decades to build this engineering marvel on the Krishna River.

Environmental Impact

The dam contributes to clean energy generation while creating a unique ecosystem around the reservoir area.

Technical Specifications & Features

  • Type: Concrete masonry dam with 12 radial gates
  • Turbines: 7 units of 110 MW each generating clean electricity
  • Water Storage: Gross storage capacity of 8.72 billion cubic meters
  • Irrigation: Provides water for agricultural needs in the region
  • Tourism: Popular destination for boating and scenic views
